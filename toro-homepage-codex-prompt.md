# Codex Prompt: Design and Build the Best Possible Toro Homepage

## Role

Act as a senior SaaS product designer, conversion copywriter, UX strategist, and frontend engineer.

Your task is to redesign and implement the public homepage for **Toro**, a trucking invoicing SaaS product. The finished page should feel polished, credible, focused, and ready for a serious production product—not like a generic template, an early startup landing page, or a broad transportation management system.

Use the existing application and brand language as a reference, but do **not** copy dashboard sections directly onto the homepage. The homepage must communicate the value of the product in a customer-facing, benefit-led way.

---

# 1. Product Context

Toro helps trucking businesses create and manage invoices.

Its central workflow is:

1. A user adds shipment paperwork.
2. Toro helps turn the paperwork into structured invoice information.
3. The user reviews the details.
4. The user finalizes the invoice.
5. The invoice can then be managed and tracked from one organized workspace.

The product is intended primarily for:

- owner-operators
- small trucking carriers
- dispatchers who also handle billing
- billing and back-office staff at small carrier businesses

The most important customer problem is not “accounting” in general. It is the repetitive, error-prone administrative work between finishing a load and producing a complete, professional invoice.

Toro should be positioned as a focused trucking invoicing product—not as a generic accounting platform, fleet tracking product, dispatch suite, ELD, or all-in-one enterprise TMS.

---

# 2. Core Positioning

The homepage should own this category position:

> **Trucking invoicing software that turns shipment paperwork into invoices without making carriers rebuild every detail by hand.**

The page should make visitors quickly understand:

- Toro is built specifically for trucking invoices.
- It works around real shipment documents and carrier billing workflows.
- It reduces repetitive invoice preparation.
- Users retain control by reviewing details before finalizing.
- It provides a clean place to manage invoice status and billing activity.
- It is suitable for small carriers and owner-operators, not only large fleets.

Do not describe Toro as a vague “business management solution.”

Do not lead with technical language such as document parsing, OCR, data extraction, schemas, automation engines, or AI unless those capabilities are genuinely implemented and approved for public claims.

Use the language customers use:

- rate confirmation
- bill of lading / BOL
- proof of delivery / POD
- shipment paperwork
- broker or customer
- invoice details
- remittance information
- unpaid invoice
- paid invoice
- draft invoice

# 3. Strategic Direction Based on the Market

Competitor sites commonly emphasize broad TMS feature lists, dispatch, IFTA, expenses, payroll, maintenance, tracking, and fleet operations. Others emphasize generic invoicing outcomes such as professional invoices, automated reminders, and faster payment.

Toro should differentiate by being:

- more focused than a full TMS
- more trucking-specific than generic invoicing software
- easier to understand than accounting software
- more credible than an “AI does everything instantly” landing page
- centered on the paperwork-to-invoice workflow

The strongest homepage narrative is:

> **Finish the load. Add the paperwork. Review the invoice. Keep billing moving.**

The website should sell relief from repetitive office work, greater billing consistency, and clearer invoice visibility.

Do not make unverified promises such as:

- “Get paid 2x faster”
- “Eliminate all errors”
- “Create invoices instantly”
- “Save 10 hours every week”
- “Trusted by thousands of carriers”
- “The #1 trucking invoicing platform”

Only use quantified claims when real evidence is available.

# 6. Copywriting Requirements

The voice must be:

- direct
- competent
- practical
- calm
- trucking-aware
- professional without sounding corporate
- confident without hype

Write for a busy owner-operator or billing employee who wants to know what the product does within seconds.

Prefer:

- “Add shipment documents”
- “Review invoice details”
- “Keep unpaid invoices visible”
- “Use consistent remittance information”

Avoid:

- “Revolutionize your workflow”
- “Unlock powerful synergies”
- “Next-generation logistics intelligence”
- “Seamless end-to-end ecosystem”
- “AI-powered financial transformation”
- “Effortlessly supercharge productivity”

Headlines should generally be under 12 words, except the hero headline.

Paragraphs should generally be 1–3 sentences.

Use sentence case, not title case, for section headings and buttons.

# 7. Visual Design System

Preserve Toro’s existing brand foundation.

## Colors

- Primary black: `#161616`
- Primary yellow: `#FFD028`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Near-white section background: `#FAFAFA`
- Primary light border: `#EAEAEA`
- Secondary light border: `#EFEFEF`
- Warm accent border: `#EFE7D4`
- Secondary text: approximately `#666666` or `#6B6B6B`

Use yellow intentionally for:

- primary CTAs
- step numbers
- small emphasis marks
- active or selected accents

Do not use yellow for large paragraphs, entire feature sections, or excessive decorative backgrounds.

## Typography

- Use `Inter`
- strong, highly readable hero typography
- clear hierarchy
- moderate line lengths
- body size should remain comfortable on mobile
- use tabular numerals only where numeric data is shown

## Radius

- buttons: approximately `10px`
- cards: approximately `12px`
- larger visual frames: approximately `14px`

Do not use exaggerated 24–40px “bubble” radii.

## Shadows

Use very subtle shadows only on:

- the hero product composition
- selected elevated visual previews
- sticky navigation after scrolling, if needed

Prefer borders and surface contrast over shadows.

## Spacing

- generous section spacing on desktop
- compact internal card spacing
- consistent max-width container
- strong vertical rhythm
- no giant empty hero area
- no cramped mobile sections

Suggested content max width: roughly `1180px–1240px`.

Allowed:

- subtle navigation shadow after scroll

- smooth anchor scrolling

Avoid:

- parallax

- cursor-follow effects
- marquee logo strips

# 17. Research Notes Behind This Direction

These notes are context, not copy to place verbatim on the website.

- Truckbase prominently markets invoice generation from rate confirmations and bills of lading, accessorial handling, document organization, unpaid/overdue visibility, and back-office automation. Toro should compete on workflow clarity while avoiding broad TMS positioning.
- TruckLogics emphasizes an all-in-one owner-operator platform covering dispatch, IFTA, expenses, settlements, and invoices. Toro should avoid becoming an undifferentiated feature catalog.
- Rigbooks differentiates trucking-specific software from generic accounting tools and highlights keeping BOLs, rate confirmations, and delivery receipts connected to invoice workflows. This validates trucking-specific language and document context.
- FreshBooks emphasizes simple professional invoicing, reduced admin work, payment convenience, and automation. Toro can borrow the clarity and outcome-led writing style without copying generic small-business positioning or claiming unsupported payment features.
- Strong SaaS landing pages generally lead with one primary promise, demonstrate a simple workflow, explain differentiated value, address trust, answer objections, and repeat one consistent CTA.

The final implementation should synthesize these lessons into an original Toro homepage rather than imitate any competitor’s visual design or wording.
